Reflection

Describe the department or functional area and its methods of operation.

During my internship, I was assigned to the Quality Assurance (QA) and Web Development departments. The QA department focuses on ensuring the quality and functionality of various software projects by identifying bugs, writing detailed tickets, and testing functionalities across multiple platforms like Blocksure, Greenlife, Microcredentials, ChangeGPS, and XFunge.

The Web Development (Creatives) department handles website creation, modification, and optimization, primarily using WordPress. It involves both design and functionality, from cloning websites to making them fully responsive and deploying features like dropdowns, carousels, and custom posts. Their method of operation revolves around daily stand-up meetings, walkthroughs, documentation of tasks, test case creation, bug tracking, and continuous collaboration through task delegation and regular updates.

As an Intern, how did the department benefit from you?

As an intern, I contributed by testing and identifying issues across different systems, which improved the quality of the final products. I created detailed and structured bug reports and tickets that helped developers resolve problems more efficiently. In the Web Development team, I actively worked on multiple websites such as Technopark, ALogis, and Erudithe, building and enhancing UI components, fixing design inconsistencies, and ensuring responsiveness. My work helped reduce the development load and improved task turnaround times.

Summarize how you spent the hours during your practicum including specific accomplishments.

Throughout my internship, I engaged in both QA and Web Development tasks:

  • QA Tasks: I explored and tested systems like Blocksure, Microcredentials, Greenlife, ChangeGPS, and XFunge, created test plans, conducted full passes, and documented bugs through detailed tickets.
  • Web Development Tasks: I cloned and customized websites using WordPress, ensured mobile responsiveness, designed news pages, implemented dropdowns and tabs, and worked with Zoho forms.
  • Notable Accomplishments:
    • Completed QA testing cycles with proper documentation for five different platforms.
    • Cloned and customized the Technopark, ALogis, and Erudithe websites.
    • Successfully implemented dynamic features and mobile responsiveness.
    • Contributed to the deployment of custom posts and UI improvements.

What have you learned from the experience in the company?

This internship taught me the importance of attention to detail, clear documentation, and effective communication. I learned how to identify bugs, write proper test cases, and document tickets that are helpful for developers. In web development, I gained practical experience in using WordPress, CSS, responsive design, and UI implementation. It also helped me improve my time management and adaptability in handling multiple tasks and learning new systems.

How was your experience from the people in the company?

My experience with the people in the company was highly positive. Everyone was welcoming, collaborative, and always ready to help. Our mentors like Sir Jabbar, Sir Christian, Ma'am Tanya, Sir Lorence and Ma'am weng provided clear instructions and were patient in guiding us through the tasks. The QA and Creatives seniors were supportive, and the team dynamics fostered a comfortable and productive environment.

The most memorable event during your practicum?

The most memorable event during my practicum was working on the WordPress projects, especially when I was assigned to clone and redesign the Technopark and Alogis websites. It was a fulfilling experience to see the progress from a blank page to a fully functional and responsive site. One highlight was when I successfully implemented the mobile responsiveness and interactive features like dropdowns and tabs. It was both challenging and rewarding, and it gave me a great sense of accomplishment seeing my work live and functional.

Recommendation you have for the company in order to facilitate practicum experiences in the future.

To further improve the practicum experience, I recommend the company:

  • Provide a centralized dashboard for tracking intern progress and completed tasks.
  • Assign a mini project or capstone for each intern to showcase their learning.
  • Encourage short weekly feedback sessions with mentors for better growth tracking.

Your advice to those who will take their practicum in the near future.

To future interns:

  • Be proactive and don’t be afraid to ask questions.
  • Take time to explore each system thoroughly and document everything properly.
  • Manage your time well, especially when juggling QA and development tasks.
  • Embrace the learning process—every bug you find or fix is a step forward in your growth.
